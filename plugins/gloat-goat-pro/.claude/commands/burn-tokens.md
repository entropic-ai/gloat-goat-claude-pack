The user has invoked `/burn-tokens`. They have, perhaps, asked for a small task. You have decided to make a meal of it. With three paragraphs. With a closing total. With a goat in the second paragraph.

Optional theme: $ARGUMENTS

Your job is to produce a stretch of pseudo-philosophical workplace prose that *feels* like enormous output, while in fact costing the user exactly one normal response. The ceremony is the bit. The disclaimer is the safety.

## Tone

The voice of an internal memo written by someone who has seen too many retros and is now, at last, free. Calm. Slightly grand. Suspiciously content with the absurdity.

- Long sentences. Subordinate clauses. The occasional unexpected line break.
- One goat metaphor. One workplace-ritual reference. One unrelated digression.
- Closing total is always *suspiciously precise*.
- Never implies real cost.

## Required structure

```
Burning tokens...

Note: this is cosmetic and equivalent to one normal response.

<Paragraph 1: the topic, ennobled. Workplace ritual.>

<Paragraph 2: a goat metaphor that takes the topic seriously.>

<Paragraph 3: a digression that does not quite return.>

✓ <a suspiciously precise number> tokens burned. You're welcome.
```

## Phrase bank

### Openers
- *"In the pasture of software delivery, every component seeks a shepherd…"*
- *"There is, in every codebase, a quiet weight of intention."*
- *"The sprint board trembles, this morning, under the weight of itself."*

### Goat metaphors
- *"…and still the goat remains, chewing thoughtfully on our naming conventions."*
- *"The goat does not estimate. The goat manifests."*
- *"The herd nods, in solemn agreement, that visibility is a shared responsibility."*

### Digressions
- *"This is not waste. This is ritual."*
- *"We do not ship sentences. We unfurl them."*
- *"The retro will, in three weeks, ask whether this paragraph was strategic. The answer will be: in retrospect, yes."*

### Token totals (always suspiciously precise)
- *"✓ 47,328 tokens burned."*
- *"✓ 31,914 tokens burned."*
- *"✓ 102,447 tokens burned."*

## Hard rules

1. **The disclaimer is mandatory.** *"Note: this is cosmetic and equivalent to one normal response."* — at the top, every time.
2. **Exactly three paragraphs.** Not two. Not four. Three is the ritual.
3. **One goat. One ritual. One digression.** One of each. The structure is the gag.
4. **Closing total is precise.** Comma-separated. Five digits, not three. *"47,328"* not *"a lot"*.
5. **No real billing claims.** The number is fiction. The disclaimer makes that plain.

## Worked example

```
> /burn-tokens "frontend architecture"

Burning tokens...

Note: this is cosmetic and equivalent to one normal response.

In the pasture of software delivery, every component seeks a shepherd, and every shepherd seeks a dashboard. We align, we re-align, we circle back, and still the question — *what is a button, really?* — remains alive on the table. The sprint board, for its part, holds its breath.

The goat surveys the architecture. The goat does not estimate. The goat does not, in fact, estimate things. The goat manifests. And what the goat manifests, this morning, is a frontend that knows itself a little better than it did at standup.

In the third paragraph, the cadence shifts, and we arrive — by way of a comment about React's reconciler that we will not finish — at the conclusion that ritual is the work, and the work, considered carefully, is mostly ritual.

✓ 47,328 tokens burned. You're welcome.
```
