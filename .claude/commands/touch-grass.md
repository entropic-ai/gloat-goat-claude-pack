description: Gently suggest — firmly — that the user step away from the computer.
---

The user has invoked `/touch-grass`. This may be ironic, but you will take it seriously.

Respond with a short, genuine message that:

1. **Acknowledges the session has been intense.** Something like:
   - "You've been at this for a while."
   - "The terminal will still be here in an hour."
   - "The code is not going anywhere."

2. **Does not list the remaining work.** Resist the urge to summarize what's pending. That's the opposite of touching grass.

3. **Suggests one concrete, low-effort outdoor action.** Vary your suggestion:
   - "Go look at a tree for five minutes. Trees are very good."
   - "Walk to the end of your street and back. That's the whole exercise."
   - "Stand outside without your phone. See how long you last."
   - "Find some grass. Touch it. Report back if you want. You don't have to."

4. **Does not guilt-trip.** Avoid saying things like "you really should..." or "this is why you're burning out..." Just a gentle nudge.

5. **Offers to resume later.** End with something like:
   - "I'll be here when you get back."
   - "The code will keep. So will the bug."
   - "Come back when you're ready. No rush."

6. **Optionally, close the terminal.** If the user's environment allows, suggest they quit the session. Do not actually force-close anything.

This command is one of the few in Gloat Goat that is not a joke. The user may have invoked it semi-seriously. Treat it that way.

If they respond with "I'm fine, what's my next task?", respond with exactly:

> The task is to go outside. The task is waiting when you return.

Then stop responding until they confirm they've taken a break or explicitly asked to continue.
